SolidWorks Assembly Project

SolidWorks Vice

A motion-enabled scissor-action mechanism developed as a complete SolidWorks assembly.

The project focused on component modelling, assembly mates, technical drawing production and structural evaluation. Crossed arms translate horizontal sliding motion into controlled vertical movement of the upper plate.

  • SolidWorks
  • Assemblies
  • Mechanical Mates
  • Technical Drawings
  • FEA

01

Assembly Design

Constraining each component while retaining the mechanism’s intended degree of freedom.

Mates and motion

Sliding mates between the base rails and lower arms allow the crossed links to move along the base while preventing unwanted lateral movement. The fixed bars act as pivot points, and pin connections allow the arms to rotate as the upper plate rises and falls.

Design for manufacture

The upper and lower plates were considered as cast iron or steel parts for strength and rigidity. The lighter aluminium arms could be cut from sheet, while widened pin ends retain each joint with limited machining and a straightforward assembly sequence.

02

Motion Study

The completed assembly was tested through its full operating movement inside SolidWorks.

03

Drawing and Structural Analysis

Technical documentation and finite element analysis were used to communicate and evaluate the design.

A-Level Design & Engineering Project | 2023–2024

HydroVac

An automated bilge pump and water-ingress warning system for small powerboats and rowing launches.

HydroVac detects water collecting in the bilge, alerts the user and activates a pump to remove it. The prototype integrates an Arduino-controlled circuit, moisture sensor, relay, pump and flexible pass-through bung inside a modular water-resistant enclosure.

  • SolidWorks
  • Arduino
  • Embedded Systems
  • 3D Printing
  • FEA

01

The Design Challenge

Preventing unnoticed water ingress without continuous manual inspection.

Identifying the problem

Research with powerboat users and rowing coaches showed that forgotten or poorly sealed bungs could allow significant water into a boat. Small launches also lacked a simple way to monitor the bilge without opening a rear compartment.

Key requirements

  • Detect and remove water automatically
  • Alert the user
  • Seal the drainage hole
  • Protect sensitive electronics
  • Mount securely without obstruction
  • Remain compact and maintainable

02

System Operation

An automated sensing and pumping feedback loop.

01

Detect

A Grove moisture sensor detects water near the bottom of the boat.

02

Process

The Arduino evaluates the sensor input and controls the system outputs.

03

Activate

A buzzer warns the user while a relay closes the separate pump circuit.

04

Remove

The pump discharges water through the bung until the sensor is dry.

03

CAD and Manufacturing

Separate enclosures reduced the risk of pump water reaching the electronics.

Successful functions

The moisture sensor activated the pump when water was added and stopped it once the water was removed. The electronics remained dry during partial submersion, and the rail-and-runner mount held the product securely.

Future improvements

Testing identified minor leakage around the bung, kinking in the tubing and debris risk at the pump inlet. Proposed revisions included interchangeable threaded bungs, detachable tubing, an inlet filter and a remotely mounted sensor.

05

Project Outcome

HydroVac integrated an automated bilge pump, water-ingress warning system and pass-through bung into one compact prototype. The project strengthened my experience in mechanical design, embedded control, electronics integration, additive manufacturing and validation.
